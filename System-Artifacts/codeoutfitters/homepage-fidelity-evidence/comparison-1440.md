## Evidence

- Approved: `source-1440.png`
- Native before repair: `native-1440-before.png`
- Native after repair: `native-1440-after.png`

## Before repair

- Hero occupied a full viewport and its staged content could remain transparent.
- Process used a tall dark treatment with no visible cards instead of the approved cream alternating timeline.
- Calculator was a compact single-panel stack instead of the approved split input/result composition.
- Case-study, testimonial, and FAQ shells used different backgrounds, proportions, copy, and card geometry.
- Integration marquee used one faster track and a different tool inventory.

## After repair

- Exact section order, dark/light rhythm, 1180px shell, headline hierarchy, responsive grids, and approved copy are present.
- Hero, four metric cells, two integration rows, 12-column services bento, four-step timeline, split calculator, three-card proof grid, featured/two-row testimonials, five-row FAQ, CTA, and three-column footer match the approved composition.
- Full-page height is 8,920px versus 9,368px for the source (4.8% difference), primarily from native font rasterization and compact demo-state content rather than missing sections.
- Runtime check: no horizontal overflow, broken images, console/page errors, hydration errors, or settled height change.

## Remaining non-blocking differences

- Animated workflow/demo content can be captured at a different point in its cycle than the approved source.
- Browser font rasterization and native SVG/icon rendering produce small pixel-level differences.

Verdict: pass — no obvious design substitution or major structural mismatch remains.
